INTRODUCTION

介绍

INTRODUCTION

意在探索“谁掌握了权利”一问，并希望将我的作业能够作为我实践过程的体现。“售后模拟器”是一个以桌面卡牌游戏的形式，对商品销售及售后服务过程进行的模拟，意在通过两个对立的玩家——“厂商”和“消费者”的互动，求得权利所属的问题。




The purpose is to explore the question "who has the power" and hope that my homework can be used as a reflection of my practice process.
"After-sales simulator" is a simulation of the process of commodity sales and after-sales service in the form of a desktop card game. It aims to obtain the problem of rights through the interaction between two opposing players -- "manufacturers" and "consumers".

目的

AIMS

我之所以选择以游戏的方式探究这个问题，首先是因为“权力所属”是个抽象的问题，我不认为我能通过设计一件具体的物件找到我的答案；其次，在我看来，游戏相较于产品设计更能体现我的个人意志:游戏的规则是不可抗拒的，而我享有设计规则的权力，可以说我对整个游戏的机制都掌有绝大部分的控制权。我建立了一个看上去设计者拥有绝对权力的环境，目的是基于设计者的立场，对权力的运行造成绝对的干涉。 

I chose to explore this question in a game way, first of all, because "power ownership" is an abstract question, and I don't think I can find the answer by designing a concrete object. Secondly, in my opinion, games can reflect my personal will more than product design: the rules of the game are irresistible, and I have the right to design the rules, so It can be said that I have the vast majority of control over the entire game mechanism.
I have created an environment in which the designer appears to have absolute power, with the aim of creating absolute interference in the operation of power from the designer's standpoint.

目标

OBJECTIVES

我希望尽力设计出一个规则完善的游戏，并从中得到问题的答案。至于游戏的可玩性和新鲜度该如何提高，这也是目标中的一部分，但这些将在一个完整的游戏出现之后再得到不断提升。






I want to try to design a game with perfect rules and get the answers to the questions.
How the gameplay and freshness of the game can be improved is part of the goal, but these will be improved after a full game emerges.

这个项目也许不尽人意，但我想我是从中有所收获的。
作为设计师，我认为我在给用户权力，但实际上所有的游戏设计都有我的权力。即便我会不断尝试，但事实证明，这是一种幻觉——我实际上总是控制着玩家的活动范围。从某种意义上来说，或许他们并没有选择。 
而这种幻觉，就是“选择的幻觉”。 
hello
It may not have been a great project, but I think I learned something from it.
As a designer, I think I'm giving power to the user, but virtually all game design has my power.
Even if I kept trying, it turned out to be an illusion -- I was actually always in control of the player's range of motion.
In a sense, maybe they don't have a choice.
 
This illusion is the "illusion of choice".

 Chen
